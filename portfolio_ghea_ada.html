Why Apple Developer Academy

A scientist who got
tired of bad tools.

In pharmaceutical research, I watched study coordinators manually transfer data from paper forms to spreadsheets — introducing errors that could delay drug approvals for thousands of patients. In radiation safety, monitoring was reactive instead of real-time. I saw the problems clearly every single day.

The frustrating part? The technology to fix all of it already existed. What was missing was someone who understood both the domain and the craft of building software.

I studied Software Engineering at university. I've used R-Software for statistical analysis. I helped define requirements for an e-CRF digital platform. I'm not starting from zero — I'm bridging from one kind of precision to another.

The Apple Developer Academy teaches exactly what I need: not just Swift, but human-centered design, collaborative development, and the discipline to ship software that actually matters to people.
